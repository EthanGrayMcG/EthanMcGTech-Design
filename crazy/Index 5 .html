General Theater











Theater has been a growing tradition of a form of entertainment dating a long ways back. In Shakespeare's time, theater was formalized into what it is today. A few standard facts and information that have been added and moved around over times are:
Theater Directions and Terms:
Stage Right- The Actors Right
Stage Left- The Actors left
Center Stage- The Center of The Stage
Up Stage- Towards the back of the stage
Down Stage- The front of the Stage
Backstage- Back behind the stage where set pieces are kept and where the actors wait to come on stage
The House- The Audience and where they sit to watch the show
The Booth-Where the Tech Director sits and runs Sound and Lights
Curtains- What goes across the stage in the beginning and in the end
Wings- Where the actors wait to come on stage from the side
Sound- Microphones and Music
Lights- Lights to illuminate the stage and the actors
Stage manager- Makes sure everything runs smoothly backstage and Actors go on when they are supposed to
Student Stage manager- A student or younger adult who will often assist the director and Stage manager with what is needed.
Director- the person who puts the whole show together and makes sure it looks good
Assistant Director-helps the Director has he/she needs it
Choreographer- Designs Dances for the musical Numbers
Music Director- helps with singing and music
Tech Director- Designs sets and runs sound and lights
